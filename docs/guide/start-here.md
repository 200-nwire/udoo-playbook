# Where Do I Start?

You don't need to read this whole book before you begin.

You need to know where *you* are right now — and this page will point you to the right door. There is no wrong entry. The framework is designed so that wherever you start, you will find something immediately useful.

::: tip If you have 5 minutes before picking a path
Read [The Manifesto](/guide/manifesto). It's the spirit behind everything here — short, no tables, no role descriptions. Just what we're trying to do and why it's worth doing together.
:::

::: info The rule that changes everything
Whatever your situation: **never write a story straight from an idea.**

Every story needs to trace back to a Feature, an Epic, and an Initiative. Not because the framework demands ceremony — but because without that thread, you won't know why you built it, whether it worked, or what to build next. That single habit, held consistently, changes more than any other practice in this book.
:::

## "We are starting a brand new project"

You have a client, an idea, or a direction — but nothing exists yet. No codebase. No backlog. No team conventions.

**Start here:** [Full Discovery — New Project →](/upstream/discovery-types#new-project)

What to expect:
- 5-station discovery process across 2–3 sprints
- No code until the Initiative Brief is signed off and first stories are Ready
- Architecture decisions made and documented before building begins
- A team that knows exactly what they are building, why, and for whom

The reward: you don't start building and then spend twice as long fixing what you built wrong.

---

## "We have an idea for something new"

You've spotted a problem, a pattern in support tickets, a competitor move, or a business opportunity. Something new needs to happen — but you're not sure how big it is or where it belongs.

**Start here:** [Idea Triage →](/upstream/idea-triage)

What to expect:
- A 10-minute decision process to classify your idea correctly
- Clarity on whether this is an Initiative, a Feature, an Epic, or just a Story
- The right starting point for its actual size

The reward: you stop debating "how big is this?" and start shaping it with the right scope.

---

## "We want to add a feature to an existing product"

The product exists. The team is running. Now a new capability is needed — something users can do that they couldn't do before. It's bigger than a story but not a whole new product direction.

**Start here:** [Feature Design Sprint →](/upstream/discovery-types#new-feature)

What to expect:
- 1 focused discovery sprint to define the Feature clearly
- An Experience Snapshot — the day-in-the-life narrative that makes the feature real
- Epic breakdown and the first stories shaped and Ready for development

The reward: the feature has a soul. Everyone on the team can describe it in one sentence before a single line of code is written.

---

## "We need to connect our work to business goals"

You're building, but the team can't clearly answer: "Why is this the most important thing right now?" or "How will we know if it worked?"

**Start here:** [Business Goals → KPIs → Initiatives →](/upstream/business-goals)

What to expect:
- A clear chain from company strategy to quarterly OKRs to specific KPIs to Initiatives
- How to pick the right KPI for each initiative
- The difference between leading and lagging indicators
- How to make the "why" undeniable at every layer

The reward: every team member, from developer to customer success, can trace their work back to a number that matters.

---

## "We just need to add some stories to an existing epic"

The epic exists. The feature is understood. You just need to shape a few more stories and get them ready for the sprint.

**Start here:** [Story Shaping →](/upstream/discovery-types#new-story)

What to expect:
- A Three Amigos kickoff (PM + Dev + QA, 15–30 minutes)
- Acceptance criteria and edge cases agreed before work starts
- DoR checklist to confirm each story is truly Ready

The reward: stories go into the sprint without surprises. Engineers don't discover requirements mid-implementation.

---

## "We have a bug or something is broken in production"

Something is broken. A user reported it. An alert fired. A customer escalated.

**Start here:** [Bug Taxonomy →](/onstream/bug-taxonomy)

What to expect:
- Classify the issue correctly (severity, impact, priority, type)
- Follow the [Incident Management playbook](/onstream/incident-management) if it's P0 or P1
- Run an [RCA](/onstream/rca-template) after resolution
- Decide whether this bug becomes an upstream initiative or a Downstream fix

The reward: the team responds calmly, learns from what happened, and prevents the same thing from recurring.

## "I'm new to the team and want to understand how everything works"

You've just joined. You want to understand the system before you start working inside it. Everything is new and slightly overwhelming.

**Recommended path for new team members:**

1. [Introduction](/guide/introduction) — understand the *why* of the framework
2. [The Lifecycle at a Glance](/guide/lifecycle) — see the full loop in one page
3. [The 4-Layer Hierarchy](/guide/hierarchy) — understand how work is structured
4. [How to Use This Book](/guide/how-to-use) — find your role-specific reading path
5. [Wrong Way / Right Way tutorial](/tutorials/wrong-way-right-way) — the most valuable 30 minutes you'll spend

The reward: you stop guessing, start contributing with confidence, and speak the team's language from day one.

## "We're a small team — do we really need all of this?"

You're two or three people. Maybe it's an internal tool, not a customer product. Maybe you just need the principles without the ceremony.

**Start here:** [Lite Mode →](/guide/lite-mode)

What to expect:
- The same thinking model, with compressed ceremonies
- What is truly non-negotiable vs what can be simplified for your context
- A clear path to grow into the full framework as the team scales

The reward: you don't feel crushed by process. You feel guided by principles — and you grow into the rest naturally.

---

## "We manage multiple products and teams"

You're running several initiatives across multiple teams simultaneously. You need to think at the roadmap level, not just the story level.

**Start here:** [Portfolio & Roadmap →](/portfolio/)

What to expect:
- How to sequence and prioritise multiple initiatives across teams
- Cross-team dependency management
- The roadmap model (Now / Next / Later) and how to maintain it
- Capacity planning: how to keep Upstream fed while Downstream is sprinting

The reward: you stop managing chaos and start managing direction.

## "I don't know which tier applies to my project"

Different projects need different levels of process. A three-week internal tool doesn't need the same ceremonies as a ministry-grade LMS with eight developers.

**Start here:** [Scale Tiers](/guide/scale-tiers)

What to expect:
- Three tier definitions — Programme (Tier 1), Standard (Tier 2), Lean (Tier 3)
- What each tier requires and what it drops
- How the Team Lead role works at each tier
- When to move up or down

The reward: you know exactly which practices to apply and which to skip — without guilt or confusion.

---

::: info The key insight
The framework has four phases. But they don't run sequentially — they run **in parallel across different initiatives**.

While one initiative is in Downstream (being built), another is in Upstream (being shaped), and a third is in Onstream (being operated). Your job is not to complete one phase at a time. Your job is to make sure every initiative is in the right phase for its current state.
:::
