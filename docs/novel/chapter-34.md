# Chapter 34 — Dina's Tuesday

Eight forty-seven AM.

She arrived the way she always arrived — coat still on, the folder instinct suppressed now, two years into the habit of the app instead of the paper. The tablet was on the desk where Sigal had put it. Sigal who now had her own screen, the receptionist view, the emergency triage system that had shipped three months ago and had changed, Sigal had told Netta in a call that Netta had written up and tagged as qualitative signal, high value, the particular quality of her mornings.

I stopped dreading the walk-ins, Sigal had said. I still don't like them. But I stopped dreading them.

Dina opened the app.

The morning prep screen.

Three things, as always. Shaped now by eight months of iteration, of Netta's calls and Tamar's refinement sessions and Lior's implementations and Yoav's quiet deletions of everything that wasn't necessary. Not forty-seven screens. Not a dashboard full of metrics. Three things, chosen by an algorithm that had been taught — slowly, carefully, through the accumulated understanding of a team that had spent a long time learning to listen — what mattered at eight forty-seven on a Tuesday morning.

Kuki — labrador, 9:15. Vaccination overdue: distemper, 4 months. Owner: tends to ask about diet. Last visit notes attached.

Double booking: 11:00 and 11:15. Both routine. Sigal aware — managing.

Overnight message from Mitzpeh's owner: he's eating again. Follow up not required.

She read it in thirty seconds.

She thought about Mitzpeh eating again — the specific relief of it, the small good news that she would carry into the morning like something warm in her pocket.

She thought about Kuki, the distemper conversation, the owner who asked about diet.

She hung up her coat.

The first patient was seven minutes away.

She was thinking about Kuki.

She was not thinking about the software.

The software had disappeared into Tuesday.

This is what done means.
